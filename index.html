About Us

A joint initiative between RAK Municipality and the American University of Ras Al Khaimah.

RAK Center for Outdoor Comfort (RAKCOC), a joint initiative between RAK Municipality and AURAK, focuses on practical applied research on outdoor comfort in the Middle East region. The Scientific program of this feasibility period primarily aims to support adopting outdoor thermal comfort solutions in Ras Al Khaimah and the region through modeling testing and implementing activities that provide various services, such as design advisory and guidelines, education, testing, etc.

In its development strategy, the Center plans to expand its scope to adjacent fields such as micro-climate management, climate change adaptation, outdoor landscape design, urban heat island reduction, Nature Based Solutions (NBS), and others as may be relevant.

Vision

RAKCOC is the first center in the Middle East to focus on a better understanding of the richness of microclimatic characteristics in outdoor urban spaces and the comfort implications for the people using them. By controlling sources of discomfort, sedentary activities, as well as the use of public transport, cycling, and walking, are promoted. The ultimate goal is to create thriving spaces that attract and accommodate a substantial number of people.

Mission

RAKOC attempts to understand the thermal conditions outdoors. Models are currently unavailable for hot and humid countries, and solutions still need to be well-designed. There needs to be a greater understanding of the physical and human parameters in these spaces and the subjective responses of end users. The center also relies on research based on feedback from facilities equipped with instruments and long-term monitoring.

Our Services

We collaborate closely with government bodies, private real estate developers, and academic partners to provide premium technical consulting.

Urban Heat Mapping

Utilizing state-of-the-art thermal satellite tracking and land surface temperatures to highlight precise neighborhood vulnerability matrices.

Outdoor Thermal Comfort Studies

Conducting in-situ field measurement metrics (PET, UTCI calculations) to calibrate architectural spatial structures to human metabolic needs.

GIS & Spatial Analysis

Developing robust multilayered geographical maps highlighting relationships between building densities, materials, and localized cooling vectors.

Environmental Monitoring

Installing micro-weather station grids to track real-time changes across humidity, solar radiation indices, and convective current changes.

Design Advisory & Guidelines

Formulating rigorous code compliance frameworks detailing ideal cool roof applications, building height ratios, and orientation strategies.

Micro-Climate Management

Optimizing wind flow corridors and ventilation aerodynamics across urban canyons to prevent dangerous heat pockets within the city grid.

Climate Change Adaptation

Preparing city infrastructure for long-term extreme heat trajectories via predictive computational forecasting across decades.

Urban Heat Island Reduction

Engineering customized targeted solutions focusing on reflective albedo changes and high-efficiency evaporative urban setups.

Education & Research

Conducting training bootcamps for practicing municipal engineers, alongside comprehensive technical publication structures.
