About Us

RAK Center for Outdoor Comfort (RAKCOC), a joint initiative between RAK Municipality and AURAK, focuses on practical applied research on outdoor comfort in the Middle East region. The scientific program of this feasibility period primarily aims to support adopting outdoor thermal comfort solutions in Ras Al Khaimah and the region through modeling, testing, and implementing activities that provide various services, such as design advisory and guidelines, education, and testing.

In its development strategy, the Center plans to expand its scope to adjacent fields such as micro-climate management, climate change adaptation, outdoor landscape design, urban heat island reduction, Nature Based Solutions (NBS), and other relevant fields.

Vision

RAKCOC is the first center in the Middle East to focus on a better understanding of the richness of microclimatic characteristics in outdoor urban spaces and the comfort implications for the people using them.

By controlling sources of discomfort, sedentary activities, as well as the use of public transport, cycling, and walking, are promoted. The ultimate goal is to create thriving spaces that attract and accommodate a substantial number of people.

Mission

RAKCOC attempts to understand the thermal conditions outdoors. Models are currently unavailable for hot and humid countries, and solutions still need to be well-designed. There needs to be a greater understanding of the physical and human parameters in these spaces and the subjective responses of end users.

The center also relies on research based on feedback from facilities equipped with instruments and long-term monitoring.

Our Team

The center’s establishment is a recent development, bringing together a dynamic interdisciplinary team comprising AURAK and RAK Municipality members. The center is situated within the AURAK campus, Building K, and is a hub for collaboration and knowledge exchange.

The interdisciplinary team primarily focuses on harnessing their expertise in measurement and modeling. The center has formed valuable partnerships with organizations such as RAK Academy, RAK Hospitality, RAK Properties, and Marjan Island.

The Engineering School of AURAK actively participates through the Architecture Department and Mechanical Engineering Department, ensuring a comprehensive approach to address the center's objectives effectively.

Ras Al Khaimah Heat Map Strategy 2026

The Urban Heat Map Strategy identifies and analyzes the spatial distribution of hot and cool areas across Ras Al Khaimah. It highlights temperature variations, supports decision-makers, and helps implement effective mitigation strategies.

The framework includes temperature analysis, air quality analysis, land cover analysis, outdoor comfort survey, population mapping, local area study, and urban environment modeling.

Outdoor Comfort Survey

This project focuses on understanding how comfortable outdoor spaces feel across Ras Al Khaimah. It considers sun and heat, wind and breeze, shade and greenery, seating and amenities, thermal comfort, humidity, walkability, safety, and overall satisfaction.

Urban Heat Map and Environmental Analysis

This project integrates environmental and spatial data to support sustainable and climate-resilient development in Ras Al Khaimah.
